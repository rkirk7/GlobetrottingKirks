In Search of the Polar Bear (Ursus maritimus)

Polar bears are an elusive and thrilling animal, and there are very few opportunities to see them in the wild. We first sought them out on our first expedition cruise aboard the Quark Ultramarine. The cruise started and ended in Longyearbyen, Svalbard (a Norwegian island far to the north). Over the course of 12 days, we reveled in the crisp Arctic weather, soaked in the stunning scenery, enjoyed the midnight sun (which doesn't set at all during the long summer), and kept our eyes peeled for the elusive bear. Ultimately, we saw seven bears--all from the ship and all from a fairly significant distance--as well as walruses, sea birds, and other maritime life.

Cruising Around Svalbard with Quark

After our experience with Quark, we latched on to the idea of trying to see the bears closer up (i.e. be able to see them with the naked eye). The best place in the world to do this is in Churchill, Manitoba, Canada. Here, the bears come off of the ice when it melts for the summer and hang out until the weather turns cold again.
